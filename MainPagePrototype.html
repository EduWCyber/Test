What Bullying Means

Bullying is repeated harmful behavior that can be physical, verbal, social, or emotional. It can include insults, intimidation, exclusion, rumors, threats, or actions meant to make someone feel powerless or afraid.

Over time, bullying can affect confidence, school performance, mental health, and a person's sense of safety. That is why early support and intervention matter.

What Cyberbullying Means

Cyberbullying happens through phones, messages, games, social media, group chats, and other online spaces. Harmful posts, harassment, fake accounts, leaked screenshots, and public shaming can spread quickly and remain visible for a long time.

Because digital spaces are always active, cyberbullying can make people feel like there is no escape. This is why reporting tools, message analysis, and trusted support systems are important.
